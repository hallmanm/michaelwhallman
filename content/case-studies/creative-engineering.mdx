## Problem

Before the engineering leadership work, I was the Creative Developer on AEO's marketing team —
the person responsible for turning a designer's pitch into a working web experience under
campaign-deadline pressure. The constraints were unusual: browser support that spanned IE8 to
mobile Safari, designers who wanted print-quality interactive layouts, and timelines measured
in days. The portfolio that came out of that era is a collection of focused engineering
problems with creative outcomes.

## Three artifacts

### Stop-motion animation engine

A sprite-based stop-motion system for marketing imagery — the designer authored a sequence of
frames, and the engine animated them with configurable timing, easing, and loop behavior. Used
across multiple campaigns. Now lives in its own repo as
[mwh-stop-motion](https://github.com/michaelwhallman/mwh-stop-motion).

### Parallax storytelling

Parallax scrolling effects for editorial pages — multiple background and foreground layers
moving at different rates as the user scrolled. The original used skrollr.js; the modern port
uses [Framer Motion's scroll APIs](/demos/parallax). Same idea, cleaner stack.

### Regex contest validator

A word-count validator built for 77kids' back-to-school contest — restricted entries to a
configured word limit with live feedback. The original shipped on jQuery; the
[modern React port](/demos/regex) is functionally identical in under 60 lines.

## What ties them together

All three came from the same posture: **understand the designer's intent, then build the
smallest thing that delivers it**. None of these are systems — they're focused tools that did
one job well, and that approach scaled into the later platform-engineering work.

## What I led, not just shipped

This era was where I learned that the technical part is the easy part. The creative-developer
job was as much *translation* — between design intent, marketing deadlines, and engineering
reality — as it was code. That translation skill is the one I leaned on hardest when
transitioning into management.

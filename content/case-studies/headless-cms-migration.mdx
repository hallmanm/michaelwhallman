## Problem

AEO ran on **Oracle WebCenter Sites** — a heavyweight, legacy CMS that bottlenecked every
content change. Marketing teams couldn't ship updates without engineering. The schema was
locked into the desktop site's needs, which made it brittle for the mobile app and emerging
headless surfaces. And the editorial experience was bad enough that content authors
routinely worked around it.

## Approach

I participated in the vendor evaluation, helped select **Contentstack**, and then took
**technical lead** for the migration. The replatforming was an opportunity to do more than
swap vendors; it was the moment to rethink the content model itself.

**Architecture work:**

- **JSON schema for device-agnostic consumption** — content modeled once, consumed by web,
  mobile app, marketing email, and any future channel
- **Content relationships** designed inside Contentstack so authors composed pages from
  reusable modules instead of duplicating content per surface
- **CMS authoring interface** designed for the actual workflow of the editorial team, not the
  workflow assumed by the CMS

**Custom extensions:**

- Built a **Scene7 preview extension** so content authors could preview media inside
  Contentstack before publishing — the most-requested workflow improvement from the editorial
  team
- Used the Contentstack SDK to integrate the CMS into the build pipeline

## Outcome

- Editorial team unblocked: most content changes now ship in real time
- A schema and content model that scales across web, app, and future headless surfaces
- Foundation for the **template generator** work that eliminated a 3-month release cycle for
  customer-facing content modules

## Technical highlights

- **Contentstack** as the headless CMS — JSON-first, API-driven
- **Scene7** for media management, integrated via a custom CMS extension
- **Device-agnostic content model** consumed by web, app, and email channels

## What I led, not just shipped

A CMS migration is mostly a stakeholder management problem. I spent more time aligning
editorial, marketing, and engineering on the *content model* than I did writing code — and
that's where the value compounded.

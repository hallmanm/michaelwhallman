## Problem

AEO's 77kids sub-brand was launching a back-to-school contest tied to a new cast of brand
models. The marketing team wanted a single editorial experience that introduced the cast,
collected contest entries, and converted visitors to shoppers — across desktop and the
emerging mobile responsive site. The deadline was tight and the technical surface was
unfamiliar territory for the team at the time.

## Approach

Through **pair-programming with a teammate**, I built a responsive, MVC-based feature that
combined editorial content, an interactive cast showcase, and contest-entry capture into one
flow. The MVC structure was deliberate: it kept the cast data, the contest logic, and the
view layer cleanly separated so the marketing team could update copy and cast without
touching code paths.

The contest-entry portion used the same word-count validation logic showcased in the
[regex demo](/demos/regex) — built specifically for this campaign and reused on later
contests.

## Outcome

- **$961,067 in revenue** generated by the campaign
- **+77.4% increase in comps** during the campaign window
- Received an **Eagle's Elite award** in recognition

## Technical highlights

- **Responsive MVC architecture** — a new pattern for the team at the time, predating the
  modern frontend framework era
- **Interactive cast showcase** with swipe controls and embedded video
- **Server-side contest entry validation** with the regex-based word counter

## Demo status

The original page relied on Flash fallbacks for video on older browsers and is preserved as
screenshots only. The regex word counter is reimplemented as a [live React demo](/demos/regex).

## What I led, not just shipped

This was an early career win — first time I owned a campaign end to end. The Eagle's Elite
award mattered less than the lesson: shipping the *right* thing on a deadline is more
valuable than shipping the *perfect* thing late.

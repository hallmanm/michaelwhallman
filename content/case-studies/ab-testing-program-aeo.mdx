## Problem

When I took over AB testing at American Eagle, it was a single engineer running a handful of
experiments through Google Optimize. The program was a bottleneck: every product team
wanted to test, but routing all experiments through one person meant a quarter of good ideas
never shipped. The vendor (Google Optimize) was being deprecated, and the experiments that
did ship were limited to client-side modifications — heavy, slow, and brittle on mobile.

## Approach

Three concurrent threads:

**1. Build the team.** I grew the AB testing team from one engineer to a fully staffed agile
project team of **25+ people** — a mix of FTEs and contractors. Hiring was only half the
work; the harder part was building the operating model. I designed a **decentralization plan**
where experimentation was individually managed within each of the six product teams, with my
team owning the platform and the practices. That moved the bottleneck.

**2. Replace the platform.** I led the **migration from Google Optimize to Optimizely** Web
and Feature Experimentation, with experiments delivered through **Akamai Edge Workers**
instead of synchronous client-side scripts. Edge delivery eliminated the flash-of-original-
content problem, reduced latency, and dramatically shortened time-to-production for new
tests.

**3. Set the roadmap with executives.** I worked directly with executive leadership and PMs to
prioritize the test backlog by expected revenue impact. Not every idea was worth running; my
job was to be candid about which ones moved the business.

## Outcome

- **$130M+ additional annual revenue** from AB testing — multiple individual experiments each
  driving >4% lift in customer conversions
- Team scaled from 1 → 25+, with 30+ contractors across six product teams under management
- Time-to-production for new experiments cut from weeks to days
- Platform stability improved measurably after the move to Edge Workers

## Technical highlights

- **Akamai Edge Workers** for variant delivery — variant assignment and treatment injection
  happen at the CDN edge, before the page hits the browser
- **Optimizely Web + Feature Experimentation** for client + server-side coverage
- **Tealium iQ** for tag management; a single Universal Data Object normalized event tracking
  across experiments

## What I led, not just shipped

Decentralizing experimentation across six product teams was the most leveraged decision —
it converted my team from a bottleneck into a multiplier. The $130M number reflects what
*every team* could now ship on their own.
